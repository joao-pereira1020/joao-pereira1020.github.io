Projects

Circulator Packaging Robot

August 2016-June 2017


Raytheon Space and Airborne Systems manufactures circulators for use in radar systems. These circulators contain extremly powerful magnets as well as delicate wire traces and surface mount electronics.

  • Our team was tasked with creating a semi-automated robotic system capable of extracting these circulatrs from a magnetic carrying platform, tranporting them, and dropping them into a non-magnetic carrier without inflicting any damage.
  • The solution contained an off-the-shelf 3D cartesian gantry robot with a custom designed and fabricated end effector which utilizes and electromagnet and mechanical seperator plate.
  • The system ensured safe reliable transport of the circulator, thus saving raytheon at least $96,000 per year by reducing damaged circulators and human labor costs.

Awards: Won 2nd place at UTDesign's Expo and 1st place at ASME Manufacturing Science and Engineering Conference.




MECH 3v95: Autonomous Robot

Spring 2017

The project was to design an autonomous basketball robot that operates on a given court marked by lines on the ground.

  • Robot had to operate untethered and with no input during competition.
  • Robot carried 10 preloaded Nerf Ballistic Balls.
  • Our robot, Dirk Nobotzki, navigated the court using two gear motors with encoders, as well as a line sensor that was placed on the bottom in order to follow the lines on the court.
  • The shooting mechanism was composed of a ruler that was fixed on one end, then flicked with a high torque geared DC motor.
  • Both the navigation and shooting components were controlled using a programmed microcontroller .

Awards: Won 1st place in class competition. Watch Dirk Nobotzki in action!
